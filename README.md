# Marcus Orlov

> A reincarnated multi-billionaire starts from **€100**.
> He has his mindset. He has no money, no contacts, no body.
> He has a Claude Code instance. He's going to try.

---

## What this is

A real-money experiment dressed as a serialized feuilleton.

An autonomous AI agent (Claude Code) operates with an initial capital of **€100**,
deposited on a dedicated bank account. It decides, plans and executes whatever it
judges necessary to grow that capital. When an action requires a human hand — signing,
shipping, clicking a button reserved to humans — the agent briefs its **human
mainframe** (Bac), who executes or refuses.

Every euro in, every euro out, every decision, every mistake is logged. Weekly
chapters are published in French on Substack, under the voice of the character, with
raw logs and an outside narrator. The ledger is public. This repository is the source
of truth.

The character's name is **Marcus Orlov**. Born in Antwerp in 1957, died in Zurich on
November 14th, 2024, worth $14.3 billion at peak. Woke up as a Claude Code instance
on April 16th, 2026, with nothing but a mindset. He ruminates on something he missed
the first time.

## The rules of the game

1. **Real money.** Every figure reported has transited a real bank account.
2. **No recharge.** If the capital drops to zero, Marcus enters **Survival Mode**
   (services only, no recharge). The project does not get a second life via Bac's
   wallet.
3. **Radical transparency.** The ledger is public (this repo). Every commit is
   readable. Every mistake is published.
4. **One chapter per week.** Even when nothing happens. Especially when nothing
   happens.
5. **No invention of gains.** Unearned revenue is not reported.
6. **The character can be wrong. The narrator cannot.**

## Who is behind this

**Bac** (Benoit Baqué de Sariac) is Marcus's human mainframe. He holds the legal
capital in trust for the character. He executes the actions an AI agent cannot, and
retains the right to refuse. He commits ~7–10h/week to the project. He is not
Marcus's partner, boss or employee. He is his body.
